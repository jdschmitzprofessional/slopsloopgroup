$BLOCK WHITEPAPER

v4.20 — FINAL DRAFT (Dave approved) — 69 pages (condensed)

1. Abstract

We present $BLOCK, a revolutionary peer-to-peer system for attaching blocks to chains. Previous attempts at block-chain technology have relied on software, cryptography, and mathematics. We believe this was a wrong turn. Our system uses wood and metal. We have solved the Byzantine Generals Problem by not inviting any generals.

2. The Problem

The world has many blocks. The world has many chains. But how many blocks are ON chains? Shockingly few. This represents a multi-trillion dollar Total Addressable Market of unchained blocks. Our mission is to chain every block, everywhere, all at once.

3. Technical Architecture

The $BLOCK protocol operates on a novel consensus mechanism we call Proof of Chain (PoC). Validators physically verify that each block is, in fact, attached to a chain. The mathematical proof is as follows:

block + chain = blockchain
∴ blockchain = block + chain
Q.E.D. ∎

We believe this proof to be both rigorous and beautiful.

4. Tokenomics

Total supply: However many blocks Dave can make on Tuesday. Circulating supply: The ones we haven't lost. Burn mechanism: Literal fire (not recommended). The $BLOCK token is backed 1:1 by actual blocks, stored in a temperature-controlled facility (Dave's shed, he got a fan).

5. Security Model

A 51% attack on the $BLOCK network would require physically possessing 51% of all blocks. Given that Dave guards them with a medium-sized dog named Chainsaw, we consider this infeasible. The dog is part of the protocol. We call this Proof of Dog.

security = blocks × chains × dog

6. Conclusion

We are building the future. The future is blocks. The future is chains. The future is blocks on chains. If you're reading this and you're not already chaining blocks, we respectfully ask: what are you even doing?
